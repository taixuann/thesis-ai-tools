# Prose Principles — Sentence-Level Writing

Load this module when polishing prose, checking for clarity/concision, or reviewing sentence-level quality.

## Clarity, Precision, Conciseness

**Clarity** — Every sentence communicates exactly one idea unambiguously. If a reader can misinterpret, rewrite it.

**Precision** — Choose the exact word. "Temperature" is not "heat". "Voltage" is not "current". Quantify whenever possible: "significantly increased" is useless without magnitude and p-value.

**Conciseness** — Every word must earn its place:

| Remove | Replace With |
|--------|-------------|
| "it is important to note that" | (delete) |
| "in order to" | "to" |
| "due to the fact that" | "because" |
| "plays an important role in" | "affects" / "influences" |
| "utilize" | "use" |
| "demonstrate" | "show" |
| "it is worth noting that" | (delete) |

## Active vs. Passive Voice

**Active** (first-person plural) is the default for analytical prose: "We measured the IV curves..."

**Passive** is appropriate for:
- **Methods section**: "The samples were annealed at 600°C" — emphasizes procedure
- **When the agent is irrelevant**: "The film was deposited via RF sputtering"
- **To foreground the result**: "A 3× improvement was achieved"

## Avoid Weasel Words

| Empirical facts | hedge ❌ | assert ✅ |
| Call causal mechanisms | assert ❌ | calibrate ✅ |

- "The device may have exhibited switching" → "The device exhibited switching"
- "The improvement is because..." → "We hypothesize that ..., consistent with XPS data"

Calibrated language:
- **Empirical facts** → assertive: "achieves", "outperforms", "yields", "exhibits"
- **Causal explanations** → hedged: "we hypothesize", "this suggests", "consistent with"
- **Comparisons** → direct: "40% lower forming voltage than [ref]"

Words to scrutinize: "very", "quite", "rather", "somewhat", "relatively", "remarkably", "interestingly" — delete unless the qualifier carries specific meaning.

## Paragraph Structure

**Claim-first**: State the claim before technical details. A paragraph follows:
1. **Topic sentence** — The single claim (1 sentence)
2. **Evidence/development** — Data, citations, reasoning (2–4 sentences)
3. **Closing sentence** — Synthesize, draw implication, motivate next

**Close every paragraph**: The last sentence must conclude or motivate — never trail off with bare citations or "which we detail below."

## AI-Writing Tell Detection

Avoid these patterns that mark AI-generated text:

**Red-flag words**: "delve", "leverage", "landscape", "tapestry", "multifaceted", "paradigm shift"

**Red-flag structures**:
- "not X, but Y" — rephrase positively
- Overly balanced "on one hand / on the other hand"
- "Building on this", "Taking this a step further"
- "Crucial", "vital", "essential" used as hollow intensifiers
