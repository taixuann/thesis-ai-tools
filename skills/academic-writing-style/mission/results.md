# Results — Data-First Presentation

Load this module when drafting or reviewing a Results section.

## Rules

- **Data-first**: Lead with what was observed, not what it means
- **Figure-driven**: Every major result should have a corresponding figure or table
- **Interpret figures, don't just reference**: Not "as shown in Figure 3" — say "Figure 3 reveals a linear increase above V_th, with a slope of..."
- **Cross-reference all floats**: Every figure and table must be explicitly discussed in the body text
- **No interpretation**: Save "this suggests", "this indicates" for Discussion
- **Report statistics**: Means ± SD, p-values, sample sizes
- **Organize logically**: By research question or measurement technique — not chronologically

## Figure and Table Conventions

- **One figure, one message**: Don't layer multiple stories onto one visualization
- **Caption self-sufficiency**: A caption must be understandable without reading the body text — state what it shows, define abbreviations, highlight the key takeaway
- **Figure-text-caption consistency**: What the figure shows, what the caption says, and what the body says must all match exactly
