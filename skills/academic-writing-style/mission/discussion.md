# Discussion — Interpret, Compare, Contextualize

Load this module when drafting or reviewing a Discussion section.

## Structure

1. **Restate key findings** (1 paragraph) — Headline results (brief, no repetition of Results)
2. **Interpret findings** — What do the results *mean*? Propose mechanisms. Connect to physical/chemical principles.
3. **Compare to literature** — How do your results align or diverge? Be specific: "Our forming voltage (2.1 V) is 40% lower than the 3.5 V reported by Smith et al. [12] for similar stacks."
4. **Acknowledge limitations** — Strategic placement by document type (see `mission/limitations.md`)
5. **Implications and future work** — What does this enable? What should be done next?

## Tone

Deductive, analytical, active voice, first-person plural, calibrated hedging. Flow as claim → evidence → mechanism → example.

## Comparing to Literature

Always cite specific values from prior work alongside your own. Vague comparisons ("better than literature", "state-of-the-art") are unacceptable without quantification.
